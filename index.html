Roseville & Greater Sacramento housing

Mortgage rates just slipped to their lowest in months, inventory is finally loosening up, and the Fed signaled it's done cutting for now. Here's what it all means for buyers and sellers across Roseville and the greater Sacramento area this morning.

AI AI Market Insight — Darien's Desk

A real summer window is opening for buyers

Three things are lining up at once: the 30-year rate eased to 6.47% (about $90/month less than this time last year on a $400K loan), active listings are climbing, and homes are taking a touch longer to sell. Translation — buyers have more choice and more negotiating room than they've had in two years.

The catch from this week's Fed meeting: don't wait for big rate cuts. Policymakers now lean toward raising rates later in 2026, not lowering them. If you find the right home, marrying the house and dating the rate (refinancing later) beats waiting for a drop that may not come. Sellers: demand is steady and first-time buyers are back — but with more competition, sharp pricing and presentation win.

Real Estate & HousingLead Story

30-year mortgage slips to 6.47% — lowest in months

Freddie Mac's weekly survey put the 30-year fixed at 6.47%, down from 6.52% a week ago and 6.81% a year ago, with the 15-year at 5.81%. The dip followed lower oil prices after the Iran ceasefire. per Freddie Mac PMMS ↗

For YouOn a $400K loan, today's rate runs roughly $90/month — about $1,080/year — below last June.

Existing-home sales hit a 5-month high; first-timers surge back

Sales rose 3.2% in May to a 4.17M annual pace — the strongest since December — with the median price at $429,300 and first-time buyers about 35% of purchases, their highest share in years. June figures land July 9. per National Association of Realtors ↗

For YouMore first-timers entering means move-up sellers have a deeper buyer pool for entry-level homes.

Inventory is rising — buyers are regaining leverage

Active and new listings are both up year-over-year, homes are selling a bit faster, and listing prices have softened for several straight months. Forecasters expect a slowly cooling but still-pricey market, with affordability at a three-year best. per HousingWire ↗

For YouSelective buyers win this summer — more options and room to negotiate on price, repairs, and closing costs.

The Zillow vs. MLS listing wars escalate

Zillow filed an antitrust suit against Compass and Chicago's MLS over private "pre-marketed" listings, while Nashville's Realtracs extended a ceasefire to keep its Zillow feed live amid negotiations. per Inman ↗

For YouWhere your listing appears is now a strategy decision — let's talk exposure before you list.

Buyers are warming to AI — but still want a human

A new survey found 53% of buyers would be comfortable purchasing without direct human involvement and 75% expect AI somewhere in the mortgage process — yet trust in AI to actually find a home fell to 16%. per HousingWire ↗

For YouUse AI for research; lean on a local agent for negotiation and the details that protect your biggest investment.
Business & MarketsMoney

Fed holds rates — but the dot plot now points to a hike

In Kevin Warsh's first meeting as chair, the FOMC voted 12-0 to hold the funds rate at 3.5%–3.75%, but the new projections lifted the year-end median to 3.8% — implying at least one hike in 2026 — as the Fed raised its inflation forecast on higher energy costs. per CNBC ↗

For YouMortgage rates aren't likely to fall sharply soon — plan around today's numbers, not a hoped-for cut.

Stocks rebound; markets closed today for Juneteenth

The Nasdaq jumped 1.9% and the S&P 500 added 1.1% Thursday, recovering from the prior session's Fed-driven slide, led by tech and small caps. U.S. markets are closed Friday, June 19, for the Juneteenth holiday. per CNBC Markets ↗

Oil slides on the ceasefire — easing pressure on rates

News of a potential end to the U.S.–Iran war pushed oil prices lower this week, taking some upward pressure off Treasury yields — part of why mortgage rates ticked down even as the Fed flagged energy-driven inflation risk. per Money ↗

U.S. and Iran sign initial deal to end the war

The two governments signed a preliminary memorandum calling for an immediate end to military operations and a 60-day window to reach a final agreement, reopening the Strait of Hormuz and allowing Iran to resume oil exports. per NPR ↗

Supreme Court enters the final stretch of its term

Roughly 22 of 58 argued cases remain undecided heading into late June. The Court has already struck down the administration's tariff program — potentially forcing $100B–$130B in refunds — and narrowed a key provision of the Voting Rights Act. per NPR ↗

The World Cup kicks off on U.S. soil

The 2026 FIFA World Cup is underway across North America, with the U.S. men's team opening Group D play against Australia in Seattle — a summer of marquee matches in American cities through mid-July. per AOL/AP ↗
