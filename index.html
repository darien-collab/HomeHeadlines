Roseville & Greater Sacramento housing

Rates are holding near multi-month lows, home sales hit a five-month high, and local listings are piling up — even as the Fed hints its next move is a hike, not a cut. Here's your Roseville & Sacramento rundown.

AI AI Market Insight — Darien's Desk

The friendliest summer for buyers in years

Rates held at 6.47% (about $90/month less than last year on a $400K loan), sales hit a 5-month high, and local listings are up an estimated 15–20%. Buyers finally have choice and room to negotiate.

But don't bank on big cuts — 9 of 18 Fed officials now expect a hike this year. Find the right home and "marry the house, date the rate." Sellers: price sharp, because competition is back.

30-year mortgage holds at 6.47%

Freddie Mac's 30-year fixed held at 6.47% — below 6.52% a week ago and 6.81% a year ago. The 15-year eased to 5.81%.

Home sales hit a 5-month high

May sales jumped 3.2% to a 4.17M pace, the strongest since December. The median price hit $429,300 and first-timers were about 35% of buyers.
